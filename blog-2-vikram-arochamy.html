A Picture I Took: With Vikram Arochamy

Building systems that think

January 1, 2026 12:00 AM 5 mins read
  • Share this:

This photograph was taken after a conversation with Vikram Arochamy, Founder of Thinksolv Technologies in my meeting room at campus, last week.

Vikram works at the intersection of data, design, and decision-making. At first glance, that world might seem far from handloom or education, but in truth, it is driven by the same questions: how do systems learn, evolve, and stay human.

Our conversation circled around the architecture of thinking and how organisations, whether technological or traditional, depend on the quality of dialogue they enable.

What stayed with me was Vikram's way of seeing technology as a thinking partner rather than a control mechanism. He spoke about how the goal of any system, whether an algorithm or a loom, should be to strengthen human judgment, not to replace it.

That idea echoed what we have been exploring through the Handloom Enterprise Forum: how design, structure, and empathy can exist together.

Reflections from the conversation

Technology and tradition share the same rhythm of learning. Both evolve through continuous improvement, each version refining the last.

Good systems are often invisible. They make flow possible without drawing attention to themselves.

Collaboration is the foundation of innovation. Meaningful progress happens when different disciplines learn to listen to one another.

In that sense, this picture is not only a memory of a meeting but a reminder of an idea: that building systems, whether digital or human, calls for the same principles of clarity, empathy, and curiosity.

Conversations like this one reaffirm that technology and culture are not opposites. They are parallel ways of thinking about improvement and meaning. One weaves data into insight; the other weaves experience into continuity. Both depend on thoughtful design and shared intent.
